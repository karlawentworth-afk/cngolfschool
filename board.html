The headline for us

If we land this partnership and deliver 5 Three Day Schools and 8 One Day Intensives in 2026, the project earns Karla and John a meaningful PGA-equivalent income each, leaves LLG with roughly £140k of business profit, and validates the school IP for future licensing. Confidence In The Numbers

The prices have been benchmarked. They are defensible.

Costa Navarino will instinctively want to push prices down. They will think they look high for women's golf. They are not. Here is how the numbers were arrived at, in case the conversation goes that way.

The prices reflect what comparable premium experiences charge across the European luxury wellness and golf market. Not what UK volume golf schools charge.

Our customer is comparing this to a Six Senses week, a Euphoria Retreat programme or a Mandarin Oriental spa intensive. Not to a £400 weekend at a Midlands range.

  1. Mouratoglou Tennis Centre at Costa Navarino itself Charges €80 per hour for group coaching and significantly more for private coaching. Our 7 hours of dual-pro coaching alone would justify €1,000+.
  2. Euphoria Retreat in Mystras, Greece Comparable Greek wellness destination. Programmes start at €400 per day, with 5-night packages routinely €3,500 to €6,000. Our Three Day School at €4,995 to €10,495 sits firmly in that band.
  3. Six Senses Porto Elounda Detox 5- to 7-night wellness intensives at €3,500 to €8,000 per guest. Same demographic, same price expectation.
  4. Bird Golf Academy USA, women's 3-day intensive Charges €3,500 to €5,500 per guest. Our pricing reflects an equivalent product delivered in Europe at a more premium resort.
  5. The Mandarin Oriental Costa Navarino guest profile Already paying €2,000+ per night for a villa. €10,495 for a four-night transformation programme is rounding error to that customer. Charging less would feel suspect.

Risk Profile

What could go wrong, and how we mitigate.

Six risks worth thinking about. None are individually fatal but they should be visible while we plan.

  • Medium

    Coach capacity

    Karla and John are the only two coaches who can run schools in 2026. We cannot run a One Day and a Three Day at the same time. The schedule has to flex around availability.

    MitigationWe have a backup plan. Several other PGA-qualified coaches in our network could be trained to deliver the LLG methodology if demand grows beyond what we two can cover. They can lead independent schools once trained, with our oversight and quality control.
  • Medium

    Cohort sell-through

    Schools may not all fill at full capacity. Selling 8 places per school across 13 schools requires meaningful recruitment effort, all of it on our shoulders.

    MitigationGolf Breaks partnership for marketing reach. LLG community of 10,000+ engaged women. Costa Navarino's own database for the One Days. Conservative break-even at 4 guests per cohort. We can always cancel a school if a cohort doesn't fill.
  • Medium

    We are not talking to the right person

    Costa Navarino is a large group with multiple decision-makers. Our existing contact may not have the authority to sign off on this kind of partnership. We could waste time pitching to the wrong level.

    MitigationUse the pitch conversation to identify the actual decision-maker. Pilot proposal is small enough to be signed off lower in the hierarchy. Full launch escalates naturally to the right level once the pilot proves the model.
  • Low

    Partnership terms

    Costa Navarino may want to negotiate harder commercial terms (admin share, exclusivity, branding control) than currently proposed.

    MitigationStandard accommodation rates and meaningful marketing reach are already in our favour. Our IP is the leverage. Walk-away alternatives exist (UK five-star options, other European resorts).
  • Low

    Guest experience inconsistency

    Running the same school across four different hotels could create variable experiences and review divergence.

    MitigationThe coaching content is identical across all four. Only the hotel-specific elements (lunch venue, welcome experience) vary, intentionally, to reflect each property. The pilot tests this end to end.
  • Low

    Weather and external disruption

    June and October are slightly weather-sensitive. Flight disruption, strikes or geopolitical events could affect individual cohorts.

    MitigationSchools scheduled across June, September and October as core dates. Travel insurance required for guests. Costa Navarino has indoor facility alternatives.
Decision Points

How we know to keep going or to stop.

Four moments in 2026 where Karla and John need to look at the data and make a call. The point of the pilot is to make these decisions easy, not difficult.

— Late May 2026

Partnership signing

Heads of terms agreed with Costa Navarino. Pilot date confirmed. Marketing creative co-developed.

Go ahead with marketing setup (funded by LLG from school proceeds), or walk away with zero cash exposure.
— July / August 2026

Pilot recruitment

Are we filling the pilot cohort with the right guests? Pre-arrival profiling underway.

If the pilot is not filling, postpone or cancel. We have lost only marketing time, not real money.
— September 2026

Pilot review

School delivered. Guest feedback, handicap data, resort feedback assessed.

If the pilot works: confirm 2027 calendar. If it does not: take learnings, no further commitment.
— October 2026

Full season planning

2027 calendar confirmed with Costa Navarino. Format expansion appetite discussed.

Commit to 2027 ambition (5 × 3 day, 8 × 1 day) and the third coach contract, or scale back.
Working Actions

What Karla and John need to do next.

This is what we own ourselves between now and the Costa Navarino pitch. None of it requires anyone else's approval. It just requires us to do it.

Decide who fills the third coach role for the pilot and 2026 schools. Check availability for September. Confirm rate.

Plan the pilot recruitment

— 04

Confirm print partner. Get pricing for personalised workbook production at 8-guest cohorts. Build into per-school cost line.
