The Headline

A real revenue opportunity, low capital exposure, and strategic validation of LLG's golf school IP. The board is asked to approve the partnership and a modest investment in marketing, materials and one additional coach for the 2026 season.

The ask Approval of partnership terms with Costa Navarino, a 2026 marketing and materials budget of approximately £25k, and authority to contract a third PGA professional for delivery.
Strategic Rationale

Why this matters for LLG.

This is not a one-off revenue line. It is the first commercial deployment of the LLG psychological profiling system in a partnership model with a five-star resort. The implications go beyond 2026.

Validates the IP commercially

The 49-profile psychological coaching system has been built, tested and refined. A successful 2026 season proves that hotels and resorts will pay for it. That validation underpins every subsequent licensing conversation.

Establishes the partnership template

The Costa Navarino deal becomes the reference case. Future conversations with other five-star resorts (UK, Portugal, Spain) are far easier when we can point to an existing partnership with a destination of this calibre.

Builds brand authority beyond the UK

Costa Navarino's audience is global. Each cohort produces testimonials, photography, social content and case studies that elevate LLG's positioning into the international premium golf travel category.

Creates a recurring revenue model

Once embedded with the resort, schools become annual. The 2026 launch sets up 2027, 2028 and beyond, with format expansion (men, beginners, juniors) opening additional revenue streams from the same partnership.

Diversifies the LLG business

The school sits alongside our existing physical coaching, digital coaching and events business. It is higher-margin, lower-frequency, and reaches a higher-value customer than our current offering.

Investment Required

What needs to be spent to deliver.

The school operates almost entirely on a pay-as-we-go model. Capital exposure is genuinely low.

Marketing & Launch

£15,000

Partnership announcement, video content, photography from first cohort, social campaign and PR push. Recoverable from cohort 1 alone.

Materials & Workbooks

£5,000

Production of personalised workbooks, training aids packs, branded materials, and the printed collateral needed for each cohort. Variable cost beyond initial setup.

Third Coach & Travel

£5,000

Contracted PGA professional cost across the season, plus coach flights and incidentals. Rooms and meals provided complimentary by Costa Navarino.

Total approximate investment: £25,000, against a projected net business profit of approximately £150,000 in 2026. Break-even is reached after cohort 2.

Risk Profile

What could go wrong, and what we are doing about it.

The board should be aware of the principal risks. None are individually material to LLG, but they should be visible.

  • Medium

    Cohort sell-through

    Schools may not all fill at full capacity. Selling 8 places per school across 20 schools requires meaningful recruitment effort.

    Mitigation: Golf Breaks partnership for marketing reach, LLG community of 10k+ engaged women, Costa Navarino's own database, and conservative break-even at 4 guests per cohort.
  • Low

    Partnership terms

    Costa Navarino may want to negotiate harder commercial terms (revenue share, exclusivity, etc.) than currently proposed.

    Mitigation: We are offering them standard accommodation rates and meaningful marketing reach. Our IP is the leverage. Walk-away alternatives exist (UK five-star options).
  • Medium

    Coach availability and consistency

    Karla and John lead delivery. A third coach is needed for full cover. Illness, scheduling or pro retention could disrupt delivery.

    Mitigation: Building a small bench of trained coaches familiar with the LLG methodology. Documented delivery system means new coaches can be onboarded quickly.
  • Low

    Guest experience inconsistency

    Running the same school across four different hotels could create variable experiences and review divergence.

    Mitigation: The coaching content is identical across all four. Only the hotel-specific elements (lunch venue, welcome experience) vary, intentionally, to reflect each property.
  • Low

    IP protection

    The 49-profile system and methodology are LLG IP. Risk of replication by competitors or by Costa Navarino itself in future.

    Mitigation: Partnership agreement to include IP protection clauses. The system itself is hard to replicate without the 15 years of profiling data behind it.
  • Low

    Weather and external disruption

    April and October schools are slightly weather-sensitive. Strikes, travel disruption or geopolitical events could affect individual cohorts.

    Mitigation: Schools scheduled across May, June, September as core dates. Travel insurance required for guests. Costa Navarino has indoor facility alternatives.
Stage Gates

Decision points the board will see.

The 2026 season is structured around clear go / no-go reviews. The board does not commit to the full programme up front, only to enable the partnership and the first stage.

— Q1 2026

Partnership signing

Heads of terms agreed with Costa Navarino. Dates locked. Marketing approved for launch.

Board approval required to sign. Cost exposure to date: marketing setup only.
— Q2 2026

Cohort 1 review

First school delivered. Guest feedback, handicap data, resort feedback assessed. NPS measured.

Continue or pause review. Cost exposure to date: ~£15k.
— Q3 2026

Mid-season review

3 or 4 schools complete. Revenue, P&L, guest satisfaction, resort partnership health reviewed jointly.

Calibrate September and October schedule. Decide on 2027 commitments.
— Q4 2026

Season close

Full season review. Net profit, cohort NPS, partnership health, format expansion appetite.

Board decision on 2027 expansion: more cohorts, additional formats (men, juniors, beginners), licensing model.
